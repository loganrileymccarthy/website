One of three suillus species found with douglas fir near the coast, the others being S. lakei and S. ponderosus. It seems the species I typically find with douglas fir is this one, but it's hard to tell it apart from S. ponderosus. The cap is quite viscid, and it has a white partial veil over yellow pores. I have found it alongside what appears to be Gomphidius oregonensis.
