Lactarius rubidus

MykoWeb
MushroomExpert
Wikipedia

The prized candy cap. Fresh, these have an interesting aroma similar to fenugreek. When dried, they they take on a different odor much like maple syrup which can be introduced into foods.

These are abundant at the right place and time, but aren't all that common. I have had the best luck in Salt Point State Park in the winter. These love the bishop pine needles, and can be spotted beneath them with a striking burnt orange color. This is a more challenging mushroom to identify than the more common edibles in the area. Be sure to check for the signature aroma, white latex, hollow stipe, and no acrid taste. See lactarius-rufulus and lactarius-rufus for more information.

Many other mushrooms species in the area can take on an appearance similar to lactarius-rubidus, but they will not have the signature aroma, white latex, hollow stipe, and brittle texture.

Lactarius rubidus will be growing from the ground, NOT from downed wood. Many small, brown mushrooms can be found on downed wood in similar evironments, so this can be a helpful indicator.
