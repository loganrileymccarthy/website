Amanita phalloides

MykoWeb

It might look like a typical mushroom to the untrained eye, but this is indeed the most poisonous mushroom in the world. Its toxicity leads many foragers to avoid the genus altogether, for a single cap is enough to kill a full-grown human. Fortunately, it has all the hallmarks of an amanita species (white gills, vulva, partial and universal veil remnants), so it would be simple to distinguish from desirable mushrooms outside the genus Amanita.
