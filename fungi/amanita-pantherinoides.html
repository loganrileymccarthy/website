Amanita pantherinoides

Wikipedia

This is our version of the European A. pantherina. It is somewhat common in the coastal mixed coniferous forests. It grows in the same environments as Amanita gemmata, and it can be quite difficult to tell the two apart. A. gemmata has veil remnants that remain protruded from the cap and distributed, while A. pantherinoides veil remnants tend to be flattened and more concentric. A. pantherinoides also tends to have a darker hue in the cap, becoming lighter toward the margin.
